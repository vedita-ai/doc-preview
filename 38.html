The Respondent for the first time in its application dated 12.06.2023, for taking on record additional documents, and subsequently in the evidence affidavit filed by RW1, Mr. Sharad Kapoor, had taken this plea of adjustment. It is the settled law that no party can lead evidence which is beyond its pleadings and hence this new plea which nowhere finds mention in the statement of defense cannot be entertained. Even otherwise, the Respondent in its application dated 12.06.2023, has admitted that the Claimant had raised various invoices of conveyance and mobilization amount paid to the assessor and the said amount was paid to the Claimant as per the discussion between the Claimant and the Respondent. Thus, it is clear that the Respondent had itself agreed to pay and indeed paid for these sums and hence cannot be permitted to take a ‘U’ turn and seek adjustment of these payments from the assessment fee paid, on the ground that they do not form part of the Contract. Thus, the additional defense raised by the Respondent is also without merits, is misconceived and rejected by the Tribunal.
