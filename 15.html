Thus, the construction put forth by the Respondent to the email of 04.02.2019 cannot be accepted. However, since as per the email dated 04.02.2019, the Respondent mentioned that further adjustment was to be made in ISPL books on account of item no. 4), that is, ‘Mismatches of candidates’, as also that Rs.2,50,000/- (instead of Rs.2,00,000/-) shall have to be adjusted on account of Coordination Registration and Renewal fees, in the subsequent mail, the outstanding sum of Rs.18,33,287/- cannot be considered to be the final balance outstanding to the Claimant as on 04.02.2019. However, as per the Respondent’s subsequent email dated 18.02.2019, as per the data with the finance, the Claimant’s total outstanding balance was Rs.16,98,370/-. The Tribunal finds that this was the final balance outstanding to the Claimant as on 18.02.2019, as per the Respondent’s books and the final figure seems to have been arrived after making the additional adjustments which were mentioned in the previous mails.
