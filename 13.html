As stated hereinabove, the reconciliation sheet mentioned in the email of 18.02.2019 has been filed by the Respondent along with the additional documents on 12.06.2023. The reconciliation sheet shows that as per the Tracker, the total share of the Claimant was Rs.99,69,748 (inclusive of TDS) out of which payment of the sum of Rs.82,71,378/- has been done by ISPL to the Claimant and the dues have been shown to be Rs.16,98,370/-. The Claimant, however, disputed this due amount of Rs.16,98,370/- by sending another email of 19.02.2019 by stating that as per his calculation, the total outstanding balance was more than Rs.22 Lakhs and thereby requested the Respondent to once again calculate his total outstanding balance, to which no reply was ever received from the Respondent. The Tribunal thus finds that the accounts were duly reconciled after exchange of emails and balance shown as per books of accounts of both the parties. Even though it is correct that the Claimant was not agreeable to the balance outstanding amount as calculated by the Respondent, however, as per the Respondent, the total outstanding balance of Claimant was Rs.1698370/- as on 18.02.2019. It is also clear that the difference in the outstanding balance was largely on account of difference in the sharing rate of coordinators fee. The Tribunal also notes that subsequent to the email of 19.02.2019 sent by the Claimant to the Respondent to have a re-look into the accounts, no further mail was written by the Respondent meaning that it stuck to its position of outstanding
