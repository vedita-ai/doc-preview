1.      The Tribunal shall deal with the first contention of the Respondent that the MoU dated 16.07.2015 has automatically become null and void on account of non-payment of the Co-ordinator empanelment fee of Rs.3,00,000/- in terms of clause 6(c) of the said MoU. It may be noted that no such plea has been taken by the Respondent in its statement of defense and this plea for the first time finds place in the evidence affidavit of RW1, Mr. Sharad Kapoor. As per the Claimant, the sums were adjusted from his outstanding amount, which can also be borne out from the Respondent's email dated 04.02.2019 wherein the Respondent has stated to make an adjustment of a sum of Rs.2,00,000/- on account of the Coordinator Registration and Renewal Fee from the total outstanding sum due to the Claimant, which amount was enhanced to Rs.2,50,000/- in the subsequent mail. Further, the Respondent's witness has also deposed in his evidence affidavit that a sum of Rs.3 Lakhs has been deducted as the empanelment fee for the execution of the MoU as the same was not paid by the Claimant even though he further also states that the Claimant is liable to pay an additional empanelment fee of Rs.3 Lakhs every year for the continuation of the MoU. Thus, there is a dispute as to whether the Claimant paid the Registration and Renewal Fee for the subsequent years. Nevertheless, the same would not make any difference for the reason that as per the stand taken by the Respondent in the Statement of Defense, the MoU dated 16.07.2015 which was initially executed for a period of one year was extended from time to time till December, 2018.
