This Tribunal agrees that the Claimant should have done proper home-work at the time of filing his statement of claim which should have been duly supported by the proper documents/invoices, but it is also clear that the Respondent has also not come clean on the issue.

Nevertheless, the Tribunal has already accepted the alternative plea of the Claimant based on the acknowledgement of his dues by the Respondent in the email of 18.02.2019 wherein the Claimant's final outstanding balance of Rs.16,98,370/- has been mentioned. As per the reconciliation sheet, this figure of Rs.16,98,370/- has been arrived at by deducting the sum of Rs.82,71,378/- from the total Coordinator's share of assessment fee to the tune of Rs.99,69,748/-. If the further payments of* Rs.2,72,913/- paid on 18.10.2019 and Rs.1,36,456/- on 10.01.2020 are added to the sum of Rs.82,71,378/-, the total payment made to the Claimant comes to Rs.86,80,747/-. The deduction of this sum of Rs.86,80,747/- from the total share of Coordinator's fee of Rs.99,69,748/- leaves the balance sum of Rs.12,28,107/-, to be paid by the Respondent to the Claimant.
