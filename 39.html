The Claimant has also relied upon the cross-examination of RW1, Mr. Sharad Kapoor, wherein in reply to ques. no. 13, the said Respondent's witness has admitted that Mr. Umesh Kumar is the employee of the Respondent Company. This Tribunal in view of the discussion made hereinabove has already held that the email of 18.02.2019 amounts to an unconditional acknowledgement of debt of Rs.16,98,370/- and there is an implied promise to pay the same by the Respondent to the Claimant. The Claimant has, however, admitted the receipt of the sum of Rs.2,72,913/- from the Respondent on 18.10.2019. Further, in the hearing of 11.04.2024, the Claimant admitted to have received all the payments which were mentioned in Exhibit RW1/D (colly.). The details of payments made to the Claimant show that after the acknowledgement of balance outstanding of Rs.16,98,370/- to the Claimant by mail of 18.02.2019, the Respondent apart from the aforesaid sum of Rs.2,72,913/-, also paid a further amount of Rs.1,36,456/-. Thus, the sum of Rs.1,36,456/- further ought to be deducted from the claim amount to Rs.14,25,457/-, which leaves the balance sum of Rs.12,28,107/-, to be paid by the Respondent to the Claimant. This Tribunal therefore awards the sum of Rs.12,28,107/- in favour of the Claimant and against the Respondent.
