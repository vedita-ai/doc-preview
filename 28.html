1971 ((1971) 1 SCC 67 : AIR 1971 SC 1482) that the admission of an existing unadjusted account by a party to the account will operate as an acknowledgment of liability as well as the admission of subsisting jural relationship between the parties to the said account. On the basis of these authorities, I hold that all the letters relied on by the plaintiff are acknowledgments of liability by the defendant.”

Thus, applying the above law to the facts of the present case, it is clear that both the emails of 04.02.2019 and 18.02.2019 amount to acknowledgment of liability on the part of the Respondent. Even though the acknowledgement given in email of 04.02.2019 was conditional since the outstanding sum of Rs.18,33,287/- was subject to further reconsideration by the Respondent, the acknowledgment given in the email of 18.02.2019 stating the balance outstanding to be Rs.16,98,370/- was an unconditional acknowledgment and an implied promise to pay the said amount by the Respondent to the Claimant could be inferred from the same. The Tribunal also finds that in the Reply filed by the Respondent to the Arbitration Petition No. 1220/2021 filed by the Claimant for the appointment of the Arbitrator before the Hon’ble High Court of Delhi, wherein the Claimant has mentioned about the email dated 18.02.2019 and the Respondent’s calculation of the total outstanding dues of Rs.16,98,370/-, the Respondent has duly admitted that the said outstanding amount was the amount that was due to the Respondent in 2019.
