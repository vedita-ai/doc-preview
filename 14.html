The Respondent, in its defense in the written statement, filed in reply to the Statement of Claim, has, however, contended that as per the email dated 04.02.2019, sent by the Respondent to the Claimant, it was the outstanding amount of Rs.18,33,487/- which was subjected to further adjustments/ deductions, as detailed in items no. 1) to 5). Thus, as per the Respondent, when the adjustment of these sums of Rs.4,93,854/-, Rs.2,00,000/-, Rs.8,38,912/- and Rs.4,04,150/-, is done from the outstanding amount of Rs.18,33,487/-, the balance amount comes to Rs.2,72,913/-, which amount was subsequently paid to the Respondent on 18.10.2019 and accounts with the Respondent were thus settled and closed. As per the Claimant, the said explanation of the Respondents suffers from fallacy since the total of the aforesaid sums comes to Rs.19,36,916/- which in excess of the sum of Rs.18,33,487/-, from which it was to be adjusted and hence there was no occasion for the Respondent to pay the sum of Rs.2,72,913/- to the Respondent on 18.10.2019. For this, the Counsel for the Claimant relies on the cross-examination of the Respondent's witness, RW1, Mr. Sharad Kapoor. Mr. Sharad Kapoor in answer to questions no. 12 & 14 has maintained the stand that further adjustments/deductions, which find mention in the email of 04.02.2019, were to be made from the outstanding balance of Rs.18,33,487/-, as mentioned in email of 04.02.2019 and also from the balance of Rs.16,98,370/-, mentioned in email of 18.02.2019.
